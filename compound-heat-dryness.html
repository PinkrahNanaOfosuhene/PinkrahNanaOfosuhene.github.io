Climate Extremes

Compound Heat-Dryness Extremes Analysis

Sanskar Adhikari; Nana Pinkrah; Riley Summers; Benjamin Woodson

Status

Project proposal and analysis workflow.

Objective

Quantify the frequency, duration, intensity, and spatial variability of compound heat-dryness events across Kentucky.

Datasets

Kentucky Mesonet hourly air temperature and relative humidity observations, 2016-2025.

Methods

Quality control, vapor pressure deficit calculation, station-specific 90th percentile thresholds, and event diagnostics.

Compound heat-dryness analysis workflow.

Temperature and vapor pressure deficit threshold time series.

Seasonal distribution of compound heat-dryness events.

Duration and intensity of identified compound events.

Key Outputs / Current Progress

  • Compound heat-dryness events are defined by concurrent high temperature and high vapor pressure deficit.
  • Thresholds are computed individually for each station to account for local climatological variability.
  • The workflow summarizes event frequency, duration, intensity, spatial distribution, and trend behavior.

Research Significance

Compound heat-dryness events intensify atmospheric moisture demand and can affect agriculture, water resources, and heat-stress risk across Kentucky.
