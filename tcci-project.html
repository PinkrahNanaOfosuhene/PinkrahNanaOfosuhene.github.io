Applied Climatology

Mapping Tourism Climate Suitability in Ghana: A Four-Decade Spatiotemporal Assessment using the Tourism Climate Comfort Index (TCCI)

Manuscript draft title and author list verified from the local TCCI manuscript file.

Scientific Objective

Analyze tourism climate comfort across Ghana using the Tourism Climate Comfort Index.

Datasets

Daily meteorological data from 22 synoptic stations across Ghana's agro-ecological zones.

Methods

TCCI computation, missing-data estimation, monthly averaging, and spatial interpolation.

Research Output

Monthly TCCI plots, heatmaps, spatial climate comfort maps, and planning-relevant interpretation.

Research Context

The manuscript draft states that understanding how climate influences tourism comfort is crucial for sustainable destination planning, particularly in regions where climate variability poses significant challenges.

This project is retained as an additional applied climatology project because it uses meteorological station data to translate climate conditions into decision-relevant information.

Technical Workflow and Results

  • Compile daily meteorological observations from Ghana Meteorological Agency synoptic stations.
  • Compute monthly climate variables and Tourism Climate Comfort Index values.
  • Estimate missing data and interpolate TCCI fields spatially across Ghana.
  • Identify favorable and unfavorable seasonal windows for tourism climate comfort.
