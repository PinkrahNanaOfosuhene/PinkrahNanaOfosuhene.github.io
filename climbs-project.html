MS Thesis · NSF EPSCoR CLIMBS

Severe-Storm Environments in the Ohio River Basin

ERA5 climatology, CMIP6 future climate scenarios, NOAA Storm Events labels, Random Forest classification, and high-performance computing workflows for regional severe-weather environment analysis.

Objective

Quantify historical and future severe-storm-supportive environments across the Ohio River Basin.

Datasets

ERA5 reanalysis, CMIP6 future climate scenarios, NOAA Storm Events, derived atmospheric diagnostics.

Methods

Python diagnostics, ERA5-to-CMIP6 evaluation, bias correction, Random Forest classification, HPC batch workflows.

Outputs

Severe-weather environment maps, trends, calibrated probabilities, and projection-ready diagnostic workflows.

Research Relevance

The project supports climate-risk interpretation for severe convective hazards by linking historical reanalysis, observed storm reports, and future climate scenarios. The diagnostic framework uses a large suite of atmospheric diagnostics and predictors rather than conflicting fixed-count claims.
