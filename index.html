Research Focus

Atmospheric Science, Climate Extremes, and Data-Driven Prediction

Severe Convective Storm Climatology

Environmental diagnostics for tornado, hail, and damaging-wind potential across the Ohio River Basin.

Climate Modeling and Downscaling

ERA5-to-CMIP6 workflows for future severe-storm-supportive environments and regional climate interpretation.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF/WPS simulations of high-impact events, including the July 2022 Central Appalachian flood.

Hydroclimatology

Short-duration precipitation frequency, IDF curves, Kentucky Mesonet observations, and NOAA Atlas 14 comparison.

Machine Learning for Environmental Prediction

Random Forest classification and calibrated hazard probabilities using atmospheric predictors and NOAA Storm Events labels.

Climate Extremes and Risk Analysis

Heatwave climatology, compound heat-dryness events, and climate-sensitive risk indicators.

Featured Research Projects

Research Outputs with a Computational Backbone

CLIMBS Severe-Storm Environments

ERA5 climatology and CMIP6 future-scenario framework for severe-storm environments in the Ohio River Basin.
ERA5/CMIP6 Projection Framework

Calibration and evaluation workflows linking historical reanalysis diagnostics to future climate projections.
Random Forest Hazard Prediction

Probabilistic severe-weather environment prediction using NOAA Storm Events and derived atmospheric diagnostics.
WRF Central Appalachian Flood Simulation

Convection-permitting WRF simulation of the July 26-27, 2022 flood event using ERA5 and NOAA Stage IV verification.
Kentucky Mesonet vs NOAA Atlas 14

Precipitation-frequency analysis comparing Mesonet-derived IDF statistics with NOAA Atlas 14 guidance.
Compound Heat-Dryness Extremes

Hourly workflow for identifying compound hot-dry stress using temperature, vapor pressure deficit, and precipitation deficits.
Ghana Heatwave Climatology

Station-based heatwave analysis focused on prevalence, frequency, duration, and intensity across Ghana.
