Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF Simulation of the July 2022 Central Appalachian Flood Event

Convection-permitting WRF simulation using ERA5 boundary conditions and NOAA Stage IV precipitation verification.

Scientific Objective

Simulate a high-impact flash-flood event and evaluate whether WRF captures the precipitation structure and magnitude.

Datasets

ERA5 initial and lateral boundary conditions, WRF model output, and NOAA Stage IV precipitation estimates.

Methods

WRF/WPS preprocessing, nested domains, physics configuration, HPC execution, accumulated precipitation verification, and WRF-Python plotting.

Research Output

Time-window precipitation maps, observed-versus-modeled comparison, spatial verification, and event interpretation.

Research Relevance

This project demonstrates numerical weather prediction workflow competency for an extreme rainfall event over complex Appalachian terrain. It complements the statistical severe-storm and hydroclimate work by adding process-level model interpretation of a high-impact event.

WRF WPS ERA5 NOAA Stage IV WRF-Python

Technical Workflow and Results

  • Prepare ERA5 forcing data through WPS preprocessing and WRF-compatible intermediate files.
  • Run a nested-domain WRF simulation for the July 26-27, 2022 Central Appalachian flood period.
  • Generate accumulated precipitation maps for event sub-periods.
  • Verify simulated precipitation against NOAA Stage IV precipitation fields.
