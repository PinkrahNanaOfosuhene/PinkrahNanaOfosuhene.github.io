About Me

Bridging Science & Impact

Pinkrah Nana Ofosuhene

I am a graduate researcher at Western Kentucky University, where my work examines how climate change is altering the atmospheric environments that produce severe convective storms across the Ohio River Basin. I work with ERA5 reanalysis, CMIP6 projections, Python-based diagnostics, and machine-learning methods to connect historical trends with future climate scenarios.

I hold an undergraduate degree in Meteorology and Climate Science from KNUST (Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology), where I also worked as an operational weather forecaster and contributed to undergraduate Python instruction. My undergraduate research produced peer-reviewed work on heatwave trends and tourism climate suitability.

Beyond my core climate research, I build practical computational workflows for scientific data analysis, high-performance computing, and machine-learning competitions. Recent work includes sign-language motion generation, wildlife re-identification, and reproducible pipelines for large geoscience datasets.
