Under Review Journal Article 2024
Historical Analysis of Heatwaves Prevalence, Frequency, Duration and Intensity in Ghana
Yamba, E. D., Amponsah, W., Pinkrah, N. O., et al.
Submitted for peer review

This study presents a comprehensive historical analysis of heatwave characteristics across Ghana, examining long-term trends in prevalence, frequency, duration, and intensity using meteorological station records. The findings contribute to the evidence base for heat early-warning system development in West Africa — a region facing disproportionate exposure to extreme heat under continued climate warming.

Abstract Published Ghana Science Association
Characteristics and Long-term Trends of Heatwaves in Northern Ghana
Yamba, E. D., Amponsah, W., Pinkrah, N. O.
Ghana Science Association Conference

This abstract reports on long-term trends in heatwave frequency and intensity in Northern Ghana, the country's most thermally vulnerable region. Findings indicate a significant increase in heatwave occurrence over the study period, with implications for agricultural productivity and public health in this Sahel-adjacent zone.
