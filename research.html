Research

Severe Weather, Climate Extremes, and Climate Data Science

My research integrates atmospheric diagnostics, numerical modeling, machine learning, and high-performance computing to understand severe-storm environments and climate-sensitive hazards.

Current Thesis Research

CLIMBS Severe-Storm Environments

Climatological and Future Projections of Severe-Storm Environments in the Ohio River Basin

This MS thesis research examines severe-storm-supportive environments across Kentucky and the broader Ohio River Basin. The work combines ERA5 reanalysis, CMIP6 future climate scenarios, NOAA Storm Events labels, Random Forest classification, and high-performance computing workflows on NCAR Derecho/Casper and TACC systems.

Objective

Quantify historical and future severe-storm environments.

Datasets

ERA5, CMIP6, NOAA Storm Events.

Methods

Diagnostics, bias correction, Random Forest classification.

Outputs

Maps, trends, calibrated probabilities, projection workflow.

Research Areas

Core Scientific Directions

Severe Convective Storms

Environmental controls on tornado, hail, and damaging-wind potential using thermodynamic and kinematic diagnostics.

Climate Modeling

ERA5/CMIP6 evaluation, regional climate interpretation, and downscaling workflows for severe-weather environments.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF/WPS event simulation and verification for high-impact flood and convective systems.

Hydroclimatology

Extreme precipitation, IDF curves, return periods, and design-rainfall relevance for Kentucky.

Climate Extremes

Heatwave climatology, compound heat-dryness events, and risk-relevant climate diagnostics.

Scientific Computing

Python, Xarray, MetPy, Cartopy, Linux, NetCDF, WRF-Python, and reproducible HPC workflows.

Previous Research

Ghana Heatwave Climatology

Historical Heatwave Analysis in Ghana

This work examines heatwave prevalence, frequency, duration, and intensity using long-term meteorological station records across Ghana. The Ghana heatwave manuscript is submitted for review, and a related Northern Ghana heatwave abstract has been published by the Ghana Science Association.
