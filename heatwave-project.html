Climate Extremes | Ghana

Historical Analysis of Heatwaves Prevalence, Frequency, Duration and Intensity in Ghana

Edmund I. Yamba; Daniel Amponsah; Nana Ofosuhene Pinkrah; Stephen Oppong; Mary J. Adjei; Cosmos S. Wemegah; Stephen Asare; Josephine Tetteh

Status

Preprint; not peer reviewed.

Objective

Analyze heatwave occurrence, frequency, duration, and intensity across Ghana's climatic zones.

Datasets

Daily maximum temperature data from the Ghana Meteorological Agency, 1961-2020.

Methods

Temperature-percentile heatwave definition using three or more consecutive days above the 97.5th percentile for each zone.

Graphical abstract for the Ghana heatwave analysis.

Heatwave duration and intensity diagnostics.

Heatwave trends across Ghana climatic zones.

Key Outputs / Current Progress

  • Heatwaves in Ghana became more frequent, longer, and more intense from 1961 to 2020, with stronger increases after 1980.
  • Heatwave intensity decreased latitudinally from north to south across climatic zones.
  • The Sudan Savannah experienced the most extreme heatwaves, followed by the Guinea Savannah, Forest, and Coastal zones.

Research Significance

The analysis supports heat-health risk awareness, climate adaptation planning, and early warning system development for heat extremes in Ghana.
