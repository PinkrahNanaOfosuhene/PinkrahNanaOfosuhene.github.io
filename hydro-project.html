A Comparison of Atlas 14 to Precipitation Frequency Statistics Derived from the Kentucky Mesonet

Manuscript submitted to the Journal of Applied and Service Climatology, based on the local submitted manuscript and cover letter.

Scientific Objective

Derive precipitation-frequency statistics for Kentucky from Mesonet stations and compare them against NOAA Atlas 14.

Datasets

Five-minute Kentucky Mesonet precipitation observations and NOAA Atlas 14 precipitation-frequency estimates.

Methods

Annual maximum precipitation, rolling-window durations, Gumbel frequency analysis, IDF derivation, and spatial fields.

Research Output

IDF curves, statewide precipitation-frequency maps, station comparisons, and design-rainfall interpretation.

Research Context

Reliable precipitation-frequency estimates are essential for stormwater design, flood-risk planning, and resilience, particularly in regions where short-duration rainfall strongly influences flash flooding. The manuscript evaluates precipitation-frequency statistics derived from the Kentucky Mesonet and compares them with NOAA Atlas 14.

The local submitted manuscript states that the study uses quality-controlled Kentucky Mesonet stations, durations from five minutes to 24 hours, and return periods from 2 to 100 years.

Kentucky Mesonet NOAA Atlas 14 IDF curves Gumbel analysis Extreme precipitation

Technical Workflow and Results

  • Compute rolling precipitation accumulations for durations from five minutes to 24 hours.
  • Extract annual maximum precipitation series for each station and duration.
  • Apply Gumbel frequency analysis to derive precipitation-frequency estimates and IDF curves.
  • Compare Mesonet-derived estimates with NOAA Atlas 14 and map spatial precipitation-frequency structure across Kentucky.
