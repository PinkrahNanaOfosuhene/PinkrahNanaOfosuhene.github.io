Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF Simulation of the July 2022 Central Appalachian Flood Event

Pinkrah Nana Ofosuhene

Status

Completed course research project.

Objective

Simulate the July 26-27, 2022 Central Appalachian flood event and evaluate the modeled precipitation structure.

Datasets

ERA5 initial and lateral boundary conditions, WRF model output, and NOAA Stage IV precipitation estimates.

Methods

WRF/WPS preprocessing, nested-domain simulation, accumulated precipitation mapping, and observation-based verification.

Six-hour WRF accumulated precipitation, 00-06 UTC July 26, 2022.

Six-hour WRF accumulated precipitation, 18-00 UTC July 26-27, 2022.

Modeled precipitation compared with NOAA Stage IV precipitation.

Key Outputs / Current Progress

  • WRF precipitation accumulations for six-hour event windows.
  • Spatial comparison of simulated precipitation and Stage IV observations.
  • Event-scale numerical modeling workflow for extreme rainfall over complex Appalachian terrain.

Research Significance

The project connects numerical weather prediction with process-level analysis of a high-impact flash-flood event, complementing the statistical severe-storm and hydroclimatology projects.
