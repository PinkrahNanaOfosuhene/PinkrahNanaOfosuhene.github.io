Climate Extremes | Ghana

Ghana Heatwave Climatology

Heatwave research grounded in the local abstract "Trends and Characteristics of Heatwaves in Northern Ghana" and the Ghana heatwave manuscript record.

Scientific Objective

Analyze historical occurrences, frequency, duration, and intensity of heatwaves in Ghana.

Datasets

Meteorological station data from Ghana, with local abstract materials focused on towns across Northern Ghana.

Methods

Heatwave identification from temperature records and assessment of temporal variability, magnitude, duration, and frequency.

Research Output

Submitted Ghana heatwave manuscript and Ghana Science Association conference abstract.

Research Context

The local abstract states that heatwaves pose escalating threats to public health, agriculture, and societal systems globally due to climate change, while research on heatwaves in Ghana remains comparatively limited.

This project examines heatwave prevalence, frequency, duration, and intensity in Ghana. A related abstract, "Characteristics and Long-term Trends of Heatwaves in Northern Ghana," is listed locally as published by the Ghana Science Association.

Technical Workflow and Results

  • Use meteorological station records to identify heatwave events and summarize their characteristics.
  • Assess historical occurrence, frequency, duration, and intensity of heatwaves.
  • Connect heatwave climatology to public-health, agriculture, and climate-risk relevance in Ghana.
  • Report manuscript and abstract outputs using conservative publication-status labels.
