Current Thesis Research

Ohio River Basin Severe-Storm Environments

Climatology and Future Projection of Mesoscale Severe-Storm Environments in the Ohio River Basin Derived from ERA5 and A Downscaled CMIP6

Severe convective storms are a major weather hazard across the United States, driven by the interplay of atmospheric instability, moisture, and vertical wind shear. My thesis develops an Ohio River Basin-focused framework that links long-term ERA5-derived environments to observed severe storms and provides projections of severe-storm probabilities through 2100.

The study develops severe-storm-supportive environmental diagnostics grouped into thermodynamic, kinematic, moisture-related, and composite parameters, then evaluates how those environments correspond to NOAA Storm Events reports and downscaled CMIP6 climate simulations.

Research Areas

Core Scientific Directions

Severe Convective Storm Climatology

Climatology, spatial distribution, temporal variability, and event-environment relationships for tornado, large hail, and damaging-wind environments.

Climate Modeling and Downscaling

ERA5-to-CMIP6 evaluation, overlap-period comparison, bias correction, and future storm-supportive environment projections.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF/WPS simulation of extreme precipitation and flash-flood events using reanalysis boundary conditions and model verification.

Hydroclimatology and Precipitation Extremes

Precipitation-frequency statistics, IDF curves, return periods, and comparison of Kentucky Mesonet estimates with NOAA Atlas 14.

Machine Learning for Environmental Prediction

Random Forest classification, calibrated probabilities, and physically interpretable atmospheric predictors for severe-weather occurrence.

Climate Extremes and Risk Assessment

Heatwave climatology, compound heat-dryness events, vapor pressure deficit, duration-intensity diagnostics, and impact context.

Previous Research

Heatwaves in Ghana

My undergraduate heatwave research examined the historical and projected occurrences, frequency, duration, and intensity of heatwaves in Ghana using meteorological station data. A related conference abstract was titled "Trends and Characteristics of Heatwaves in Northern Ghana."

This work is connected to my broader interest in climate extremes, public-health risk, and climate-sensitive decision support in West Africa.
