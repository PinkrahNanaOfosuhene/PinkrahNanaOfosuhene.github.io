About Me

Bridging Science & Impact

Pinkrah Nana Ofosuhene

Graduate researcher at Western Kentucky University studying how climate change is reshaping severe-storm environments across the Ohio River Basin. Research spans atmospheric dynamics, ERA5 reanalysis diagnostics, CMIP6 future projections, and machine-learning classification of severe-weather environments.

Undergraduate training in Meteorology and Climate Science at KNUST (Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology), with published research on heatwave trends and tourism climate suitability. Additional experience in operational weather forecasting and Python-based instruction for undergraduate students.

Research focus: quantifying how thermodynamic and kinematic storm environments have evolved historically and how they are projected to shift under mid- and late-21st-century warming scenarios, with direct applications to severe-weather preparedness.
