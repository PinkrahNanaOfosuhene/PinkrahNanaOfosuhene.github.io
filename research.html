My Research

Research focused on the climatology of extreme weather — severe-storm environments across the Ohio River Basin and heat stress across West Africa — under both historical and projected future climate conditions.

Active · 2025–Present Western Kentucky University NSF EPSCoR

Climatological & Future Projections of Severe-Storm Environments,
Ohio River Basin

This MS thesis project examines multi-decadal changes in severe-storm environments across Kentucky and the broader Ohio River Valley. Under the supervision of Dr. Xingang Fan, the work applies Python-based diagnostic tools to ERA5 reanalysis data, establishing a historical baseline that is then extended through CMIP6 future-scenario projections.

The central objective is to characterize how key convective parameters — CAPE, CIN, bulk wind shear, storm-relative helicity, and the Significant Tornado Parameter — have evolved over recent decades and are projected to shift under mid- and high-emission scenarios through the end of the century.

Graduate Thesis Work

In Progress · WKU MS Thesis

Climatological & Future Projections of Severe-Storm Environments in the Ohio River Basin

The thesis establishes a comprehensive climatological baseline of severe-storm environments across the Ohio River Basin, integrating historical ERA5 diagnostics with CMIP6 ensemble projections under SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5 forcing scenarios.

Key outputs include spatiotemporal frequency maps, long-term trend analyses, and probabilistic future-scenario datasets designed to inform regional severe-weather preparedness and climate adaptation planning.

Historical Heatwave Analysis — Ghana
KNUST · 2023–2024 · Advisor: Dr. Yamba Edmund

An analysis of historical heatwave prevalence, frequency, duration, and intensity across Ghana using long-term meteorological station records. The study identified significant trends in heatwave metrics and documented the 2024 anomalous nighttime warming event, which was subsequently linked to a broader regional heat episode.

A companion sub-study documented long-term heatwave trends in Northern Ghana, connecting regional patterns to the 2024 West African heat event and highlighting the need for stronger early-warning capacity across the region.

Tourism Climate Comfort Index (TCCI) — Ghana
KNUST · 2021–2024

A spatiotemporal assessment of tourism climate suitability across Ghana spanning six decades (1961–2020), using the Tourism Climate Comfort Index (TCCI). The study evaluated seasonal and zonal patterns in climatic comfort across Ghana's four agro-ecological regions, with findings relevant to climate-adaptive tourism planning and policy.

Extreme Precipitation Probability — Kentucky
WKU · Fall 2025

A climatological analysis of extreme precipitation probability for Kentucky, benchmarked against NOAA Atlas 14 using a decade of Kentucky Mesonet observations (2015–2024). The study derived intensity-duration-frequency curves for return periods of 2–100 years and identified systematic differences between Mesonet-based estimates and existing Atlas 14 values.
