Climate Data Science

Compound Heat-Dryness Extremes

Observational, station-based analysis of compound heat-dryness events using Kentucky Mesonet hourly data.

Scientific Objective

Quantify the frequency, duration, and intensity of compound heat-dryness events in Kentucky.

Datasets

Kentucky Mesonet temperature, relative humidity, and precipitation data at hourly resolution, with a 2016-2025 study period in the proposal draft.

Methods

Quality control, vapor pressure deficit derivation, station-specific percentile thresholds, rolling-window precipitation deficits, and event diagnostics.

Research Output

Workflow schematic, event catalog, threshold time series, seasonality, duration-intensity plots, and NOAA impact context.

Project Definition

The local project draft defines compound climate extremes as simultaneous or sequential occurrence of multiple climatic stressors whose combined impacts exceed those of individual events. In this project, a compound heat-dryness event is defined using high temperature and high vapor pressure deficit thresholds.

Vapor pressure deficit is used because it directly represents atmospheric moisture demand and connects heat stress to plant-water stress and drought-relevant conditions.

Technical Workflow and Results

  • Acquire and quality-control Kentucky Mesonet hourly station observations.
  • Derive vapor pressure deficit from temperature and relative humidity.
  • Define compound events using station-specific percentile thresholds for heat and dryness.
  • Summarize event frequency, seasonality, duration, intensity, and NOAA impact context.
