Flagship Project

Active · 2025–Present Thesis NCAR HPC

Severe-Storm Environment Diagnostics — Ohio River Basin

Built a full Python pipeline to extract, process, and visualize ERA5 reanalysis variables relevant to severe convective storm environments. The pipeline computes composite parameters (STP, CAPE/CIN, shear vectors, helicity) and generates spatiotemporal trend maps for the Ohio River Basin across multiple decades.

Extended the historical analysis to CMIP6 ensemble runs under SSP2-4.5 and SSP5-8.5 to produce probabilistic future-scenario maps of storm-environment frequency and intensity.

Derived IDF curves from 10 years of Kentucky Mesonet data (2015–2024) using Gumbel extreme-value statistics. Compared statewide precipitation-frequency estimates against NOAA Atlas 14. Found KYMN exceeds Atlas 14 by 5–15% at short durations.
